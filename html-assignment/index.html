Exploring the Natural State’s Wild Side

Arkansas truly lives up to its nickname, The Natural State, offering diverse outdoor experiences in the Midwest and South. From the ancient, rolling Ozark Mountains to the challenging Ouachita National Forest, the opportunities for adventure are vast. This guide covers some of the most essential destinations and activities for the serious adventurer. It is imperative that all visitors practice Leave No Trace principles to preserve these valuable ecosystems.

Buffalo National River Region

Established in 1972, the Buffalo River is a premier destination for paddling and hiking. Its towering limestone bluffs, pristine waters, and accessible gravel bars make it a premier spot for multi-day paddling trips and scenic hiking. Many veteran explorers consider the Buffalo a must-do trip, especially during the spring float season.

Key Hiking Trails

The Lost Valley Trail is beginner-friendly, while Ponca offers more challenging elevation changes. The area around Jasper, Arkansas, and Ponca offers incredible access to trails leading to panoramic views. For endurance athletes preparing for events like the Grand Canyon Rim-to-Rim, more challenging options are available. The primary difficulty in these mountainous areas is often the rapid change in elevation.

Outdoor Terms and Definitions
Crag
A steep rock face used for climbing.
Belay
Technique to control rope and prevent falls.
Thru-Hike
A complete, end-to-end journey on a long-distance trail, often taking weeks or months.
